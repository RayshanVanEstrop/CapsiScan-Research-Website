Project Scope

Literature Survey

One of the popular vegetable consumed in Sri Lanka for many local and chinese cuisines.

In past years efficient and sustainable agriculture practices have grown exponentially. As a result, the automation of processes in greenhouses using robotics has grown as an effective solution. The usage of robotic rovers has gained considerable attention due to their potential to revolutionize farming operations. The agricultural sector can increase productivity significantly while reducing its reliance on manual labor by using robots for tasks like picking fruit and vegetables, identifying diseases and pests, checking the soil, and applying pesticides [2].

Agricultural development is vital for a country's economy and resilience to global crises. Greenhouse-based farming has enabled agriculture in various countries, including Sri Lanka, regardless of natural resources. Sri Lankan farmers are increasingly adopting modern greenhouse-based controlled farming methods. Capsicums, used in Sri Lankan and Chinese cuisine, are a popular crop. Sri Lanka produces about 36,000 metric tons [1] of capsicum annually, primarily satisfying local demand. Various types of Capsicum annum, like bell pepper and hot pepper, are grown. The crop is cultivated year-round across different districts, with major cultivation in Nuwara-Eliya, Badulla, Kurunegala, Puttalam, and Anuradhapura. Greenhouse farming offers better control over environmental factors, and technological advancements are transforming traditional farming methods, making them more efficient and effective, especially in controlled environments like greenhouses.

Research Gap

Following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent researches.

Autonomous Detection, Identification & Classification

There are no records of an autonomous robotic solution developed for data collection, real-time analysis of capsicum yield and related diseases, in Sri Lanka. Some have developed mobile application solutions for other types of crops while they had a lot of manual or human intervention for image capturing.

Risk Assessment & Remediation

Solutions for identification and classification of common capsicum plat related disease types while taking then and there remediation actions were lack in previous solutions.

Insightful Reporting

Real time reporing using a user-friendly and simple anywhere-accessible platform-independent interface for growers and owners to get information related to their farms was a priority enabling them to take more effective informed decisions.

Research Problem & Solution

Research Problem

What are the issues we face currently?

  • Lack of efficient and reliable autonomous monitoring system for capsicum (Malu Miris) plants in greenhouses
  • Inability to detect and respond to plant diseases or environmental changes in an efficient and timely manner
  • Unknowingly leading to decreased crop yields and unbearable financial losses for farmers or owners
  • No centralized system to get insights regarding the possible plant diseases and possible yield of capsicum cultivation
  • Owner or farmers having not enough information and time to plan their businesses and related strategies for growth

Product Demonstration - Solution

Proposed Solution

CapsiScan solution utilizes visuals based machine learning models for yield estimation and disease identification. YOLOv8 is implemented for capsicum plant/potted-plant locaization, plant height determination and pod counting for yield estimation. A custom CNN model is developed after researching and testing to cater this specific requirement of capsicum crop based disease identification and classification. Supporting the machine learning image processing models a robotic arm and an autonomously navigating rover are developed. Navigation uses LIDAR based visualization, Robotic OS v2 and robotic mechanics for operation. The arm operates using serial signals that are preset and contains the camera for image processing with the medicine spraying mechanism for real-time disease remediation. The system collectively works towards achieving the common goal of helping growers together with the reporting web application.

Research Objectives

1.

To use computer vision and robotic system to accurately estimate the possible yield of capsicum pods in crops/greenhouses while helping farmers and agricultural groups make informed decisions.

2.

To design and develop an automated system for disease detection in capsicum plants grown in greenhouse environments using image processing and machine learning

3.

To design an economically efficient, cooperative, multi-dimensional robotic arm equipped with a camera and sanitation sprayer for meticulous disease identification and subsequent remedial actions. This innovation facilitates enhanced monitoring and management of specific crops, thereby ensuring superior precision and effectiveness.

4.

To navigate autonomously in a greenhouse environment by using robust sensors and data collection devices to map the environment in real-time, while avoiding obstacles and accounting for the environment's dynamic nature.

Methodology

Figure 1. High Level Architecture of the system.

Figure 2. Robot Design

The proposed robotic system consists of 5 main components. They are;

  1. Yield Estimation
  2. Disease Identification
  3. Robotic Arm
  4. Autonomous Navigation
  5. Reporting Application

The system includes a pod detection component using the YOLOv8 model to identify capsicum pods on trees. It involves collecting and augmenting a dataset and fine-tuning the model for improved pod identification.

Once the robot reaches its designated location, it utilizes custom capsicum plant detection and pre-trained pot detection models to check for the plant's presence. The system then scans the plant by capturing images at different heights, identifying diseases with a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN), and counting pods with a custom YOLOv8 model, enhancing accuracy through averaging and decimal correction.

The disease detection process creates a dataset of healthy and diseased capsicum plant images, classifying them with a CNN model. Transfer learning refines the model for accurate disease identification.

The robot's navigation relies on Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM) with LIDAR, camera data, and wheel encoders to move autonomously, ensuring it stops at plants for investigation and treatment. The closed-loop system with Proportional-Integral-Derivative (PID) control enhances accuracy in navigation.

The robotic arm is height-adjustable and features a camera for image processing and a sprayer for targeted treatment. It uses a bearing mechanism to reduce friction, allowing for smooth and controlled movement during plant investigation and treatment.
